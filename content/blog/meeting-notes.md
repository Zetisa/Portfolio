## My "Meeting Notes are Useless" Phase
Before my internship, I thought meeting notes were just something secretaries took. Then I had my first team meeting, and 3 days later couldn't remember what I was supposed to do. That's when I learned: meeting notes are lifesavers!

Meeting notes are written records of what happens in a meeting: who attended, what was discussed, what decisions were made, and what tasks came out of it. During my AirNav Indonesia internship, I took notes for 12+ meetings — they saved me when I forgot what I was supposed to do, and kept my team aligned.

## Choosing a Note-Taking Tool (My Trial and Error)
I tried 3 tools during my internship, here's what I found:

1. **Paper Notebook** - Good for quick sketches, bad for sharing with team. I lost mine once and panicked!
2. **Notion** - Fancy but overkill for simple notes. I spent more time formatting than taking notes.
3. **Markdown in VS Code** - My winner! Fast, simple, and I could commit them to GitHub.

**My recommendation**: Start with a simple Markdown file in VS Code — it's free, fast, and you can save it with your project.

## The Template That Changed Everything
I used this template for every internship meeting — it's foolproof:

```markdown
# Meeting: Weekly Sync
Date: 2025-10-15
Attendees: Joel, Manager, Team Member

## Agenda
- Review last week's action items
- Discuss portfolio progress

## Discussion
- Portfolio 50% complete
- 9 blog posts needed by Friday

## Decisions Made
- Extend deadline to next Monday
- Use Hermes Agent for drafts

## Action Items
- [ ] Joel: Write VS Code post (Due: Friday)
- [ ] Manager: Review design (Due: Thursday)
```

I remember my first notes were a wall of text. This template changed everything!

## Taking Notes During the Meeting
Here's my process:

1. **Before Meeting**: Open template, fill in Date, Attendees, Agenda
2. **During Meeting**: Type key points under Discussion, Decisions, Action Items. Don't write every word!
3. **After Meeting**: Review notes, check off completed action items from last time

**Tip**: Don't write every word — just decisions, action items, and key discussion points. I used abbreviations: "w/" for "with", "b/c" for "because".

## Sharing Notes (Don't Keep Them to Yourself!)
I shared notes in 3 ways:

1. **GitHub** - Committed notes to my portfolio repo under `meeting-notes/` folder
2. **Slack** - Pasted action items to the team Slack channel for quick visibility
3. **Email** - Sent weekly summary to manager (old school but effective!)

[Picture: GitHub repository showing committed meeting notes in a dedicated folder]

## What I Learned (The Hard Way)
Mistakes I made:

1. **Writing too much** - You're not a court reporter. Focus on decisions and action items, not every "um" and "ah". I filled 3 pages once for a 30-minute meeting!
2. **Not sharing notes** - Notes are useless if only you have them. Share them within 24 hours of the meeting.
3. **Forgetting action items** - Always assign an owner and due date. "Someone should do X" never gets done!

## Why It Matters
Meeting notes went from "boring secretary task" to my secret weapon for staying organized. If I could go from messy notebook scribbles to a clean Markdown system in 2 weeks, you can too.

Create a meeting notes template in VS Code, use it for your next meeting, and share the notes with your team. You'll feel that organization boost I felt!
